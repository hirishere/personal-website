in middle school, robotics was life. this was our final robot game my last year. i was the coding lead of an all-girls team for two years. my team made it to state both years, and in my last year, earned a spot at the (global) open invitationals.

my team won an award for teamwork at the open invitational!

in tenth grade, i applied, was accepted, and commited to a two-year STEM residential high school for eleventh and twelfth grade. i thrived living with my peers in a like-minded community that provided unmatched academic and growth opportunity.

i explored as many niches as possible. alongside taking advanced courses across academic subjects, i was involved and became a student leader through my roles as a student ambassador and FBLA chapter president.

despite all i learned, i still love to code! i wanted to major in computer science and learn more, but strive to do something with impact. i took the leap to move to boston and attend northeastern university, where i am now.
